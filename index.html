Services

RML helps you design, stabilize, and maintain the systems you live and work with. Plain language, documented changes, and no surprise complexity.

Managed IT & Embedded Support

Ongoing support for small teams and busy households. Device setup, updates, backups, troubleshooting, and long-term planning so your tech quietly works in the background.

AV & Smart Home Integration

Design and fine-tuning for home theaters, distributed audio, conference rooms, and smart home devices. Clean wiring, logical remotes, and clear documentation.

Security, Wi-Fi & Cameras

Secure, reliable Wi-Fi, router and firewall configuration, and camera systems that are actually usable. RML focuses on safety, not scare tactics.

Web & Cloud Consulting

Lightweight websites, email and domain setup, cloud storage, and collaboration tools. Help choosing platforms that fit your size and budget.

Training, Tutoring & Accessibility

One-on-one or small group sessions that move at your pace. Support for older adults, neurodivergent users, and anyone who learns better with patient guidance.

Asset Management & Logistics

Inventory, labeling, lifecycle tracking, and move/refresh planning. Keep hardware, cables, and accounts organized instead of scattered across drawers and inboxes.

About Rocky Mountain Logic

Rocky Mountain Logic is led by Jeremiah Cullison, a senior technology consultant with years of experience in enterprise IT, AV integration, and hands-on support for homes and small organizations. Jeremiah approaches each environment like a system: networks, devices, audio, and people all have to work together for the experience to feel calm and predictable.

He has deep experience with accessibility and adaptive technology, supporting disabled and underserved users, and teaching at a pace that feels safe and respectful. RML is intentionally small: you get direct access to the person doing the work, clear explanations, and decisions made with your long-term stability in mind.

Core values

  • ClarityPlain language, shared understanding, and written notes.
  • ReliabilitySystems that stay stable, not “impressive when a demo runs.”
  • AccessibilityRespect for different bodies, brains, and learning styles.
  • CollaborationDecisions made with you, not dropped on you.
